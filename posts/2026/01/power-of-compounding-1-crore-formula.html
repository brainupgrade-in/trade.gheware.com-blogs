Quick Answer: What is the Power of Compounding?

Compound interest is interest earned on both your principal AND previously accumulated interest, creating exponential rather than linear growth. At 12% returns, a Rs 10,000 monthly SIP grows to approximately Rs 1 crore in 20 years - with only Rs 24 lakh invested. The Rule of 72 tells you money doubles every 6 years at 12% (72/12=6). Starting at age 25 instead of 35 with the same Rs 5,000/month SIP results in Rs 3.25 crore vs Rs 95 lakh - a Rs 2.30 crore difference.

  • Starting Age Matters: Age 25 vs 35 with Rs 5,000/month SIP = Rs 3.25 crore vs Rs 95 lakh at retirement

What is Compound Interest? (Simple Explanation)

Compound interest is earning returns on your returns. Unlike simple interest where you only earn on your original investment, compounding means last year's gains also start earning for you.

Think of it like a snowball rolling down a hill. It starts small, but as it rolls, it picks up more snow. The bigger it gets, the more snow it picks up with each roll. Your money works the same way - the more you have, the more you earn, which gives you even more to earn on.

Key Insight: Albert Einstein allegedly called compound interest the "eighth wonder of the world" and said, "He who understands it, earns it... he who doesn't... pays it." SEBI uses this quote in their official investor education materials.

Simple Interest vs Compound Interest: The Rs 1 Lakh Example

Factor Simple Interest Compound Interest
Principal Rs 1,00,000 Rs 1,00,000
Rate 10% p.a. 10% p.a.
Time 20 years 20 years
Final Amount Rs 3,00,000 Rs 6,72,750
Difference Rs 3,72,750 more with compounding!

That's 2.24x more wealth just from letting your returns earn returns. And this gap widens dramatically over longer periods.

What This Means For You:
  • If you're keeping money in savings account: You're earning simple interest at 3-4% - compounding barely works
  • If you have FDs: Interest is compounded quarterly, but at 7% rate the magic is limited
  • If you invest in equity: At 12-15% CAGR, compounding becomes truly powerful over 15+ years

The Rule of 72: Your Quick Doubling Calculator

The Rule of 72 is the simplest way to estimate how long your money takes to double. Just divide 72 by your expected annual return rate.

The Rule of 72 Formula

Years to Double = 72 / Annual Return Rate

Example: At 12% returns, money doubles in 72/12 = 6 years

Rule of 72 Applied to Indian Investments

Investment Type Expected Return Years to Double Doublings in 30 Years
Bank FD 6-7% 10-12 years 2.5-3x
PPF 7.1% 10 years 3x
Debt Mutual Funds 7-8% 9-10 years 3x
Balanced Funds 10-12% 6-7 years 4-5x
Equity Mutual Funds 12-15% 5-6 years 5-6x (32x original!)
Key Insight: At 12% returns, your money doubles every 6 years. That's 5 doublings in 30 years = 32x your original investment. At 6% (FD rate), you only get 2.5 doublings = 8x. The difference between 8x and 32x is the "cost" of playing it safe.

Beyond Rule of 72: Rules of 114 and 144

SEBI's investor education materials also highlight two related rules:

Rule of 114

Time to TRIPLE money

114 / Rate = Years

At 12% = 9.5 years

Rule of 144

Time to QUADRUPLE money

144 / Rate = Years

At 12% = 12 years

IMPORTANT CAVEAT: Survivorship Bias

These examples are inspirational but misleading if used to encourage stock picking:

  • For every Wipro, thousands of companies went bankrupt
  • Research shows only 42.1% of stocks beat Treasury bills over their lifetime
  • Just 86 out of 26,000 stocks created half of all market value creation

The lesson is about TIME and PATIENCE, not stock picking. Index funds provide compounding benefits without stock selection risk.

When Does Most Growth Happen?

This is the insight that stops people from quitting too early. Over 50% of wealth in a 20-year SIP is generated in the final 5 years.

Year Range Approx % of Final Corpus What Happens
Years 1-5 ~8% Foundation building - seems slow
Years 6-10 ~15% Compounding starts showing
Years 11-15 ~25% Snowball getting bigger
Years 16-20 ~52% The magic years - exponential growth
Why Most People Quit Too Early:

After 5 years of SIP, your corpus is only 8% of what it will eventually be. Many investors look at this "slow" growth and give up, not realizing that 52% of their wealth was going to come in the final 5 years. The bamboo tree analogy is real - growth is invisible for years, then explosive.

Warren Buffett made 99% of his $100+ billion fortune AFTER his 50th birthday. Compounding doesn't just work - it accelerates over time.

What This Means For You:
  • If you're 5-7 years into SIP: You're in the "foundation" phase - don't get discouraged by slow visible growth
  • If you're tempted to stop: The biggest gains are ahead, not behind - quitting now is the worst timing
  • If you're 10+ years in: Congratulations, you're entering the acceleration phase

Common Compounding Mistakes to Avoid

Mistake #1: Expecting 15%+ Returns Guaranteed

Why it's a problem: A 2025 academic study found 20-year SIP CAGR in NIFTY 50 was actually 6.7% (pre-tax), significantly lower than industry-advertised 12-15%

Better approach: Use 10-12% as realistic expectation, not 15%. Better to be pleasantly surprised than disappointed.

Mistake #2: Trying to Pick the "Next Wipro"

Why it's a problem: For every Wipro (47% CAGR), thousands of companies went bankrupt. Only 42.1% of stocks beat Treasury bills over their lifetime.

Better approach: Use index funds (Nifty 50, Nifty Next 50) for guaranteed diversification. Let the market do the picking.

Mistake #3: Stopping SIP During Market Crashes

Why it's a problem: Crashes are when you buy units at lower NAV - stopping means missing the best buying opportunities

Better approach: "I kept my SIP going through both 2008 and 2020 crashes - compounding and rupee cost averaging made me grateful for not pausing." - Reddit investor

Mistake #4: Quitting After 5-7 Years Because "It's Not Growing"

Why it's a problem: Only 8% of your eventual corpus is visible after 5 years. 52% comes in the final 5 years.

Better approach: Commit to 15-20 years minimum. Set calendar reminders to NOT check portfolio more than quarterly.

Mistake #5: Ignoring Tax Impact on Compounding

Why it's a problem: STCG at 20% and LTCG at 12.5% (above Rs 1.25 lakh) reduce effective compounding. Frequent trading triggers taxable events.

Better approach: Hold investments for 12+ months. Rs 1.25 lakh LTCG exemption means first Rs 10+ lakh of gains are practically tax-free most years.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is compound interest and how does it work?

Compound interest is interest earned on both the principal amount and previously accumulated interest, creating exponential growth over time. Unlike simple interest (calculated only on principal), compounding makes your money grow faster. For example, Rs 1 lakh at 10% simple interest becomes Rs 3 lakh in 20 years, but at 10% compound interest becomes Rs 6.72 lakh.

What is the Rule of 72 in investing?

The Rule of 72 is a quick formula to estimate how long your investment will take to double. Simply divide 72 by your expected annual return rate. At 12% returns, your money doubles in 72/12 = 6 years. At 8% returns, it takes 72/8 = 9 years to double. This rule helps visualize long-term compounding potential.

How much SIP is needed to reach Rs 1 crore?

At 12% expected returns, you need approximately Rs 10,000 monthly SIP for 20 years to reach Rs 1 crore (total investment of only Rs 24 lakh). At Rs 5,000/month, you'd reach about Rs 50 lakhs in 20 years. Starting earlier dramatically reduces the required monthly amount - at age 25, about Rs 1,550/month can reach Rs 1 crore by 60.

Why does starting early matter so much for compounding?

Time is the most powerful factor in compounding. Starting SIP at age 25 vs 35 with Rs 5,000/month results in Rs 3.25 crore vs Rs 95 lakh at age 60 - a Rs 2.30 crore difference. The extra time allows for more doubling cycles and exponential growth. Every 5-year delay roughly doubles the required SIP amount.

Is the power of compounding guaranteed in equity mutual funds?

No investment return is guaranteed. However, NIFTY 50 has historically never given negative returns over any 10-year period since 1991. For compounding to work effectively, you need to stay invested through market cycles and have realistic return expectations of 10-12% for equity over 15-20 years.

When does most growth happen in a long-term SIP?

Over 50% of the wealth in a 20-year SIP is generated in the final 5 years due to exponential compounding. Years 1-5 contribute only about 8% of final corpus, while years 16-20 contribute approximately 52%. This is why patience in early years is essential - the visible growth comes late.

What is the Rs 1 crore SIP formula for different ages?

At 12% returns until age 60: Start at 25 = Rs 1,550/month; Start at 30 = Rs 2,850/month; Start at 35 = Rs 5,300/month; Start at 40 = Rs 10,000/month; Start at 45 = Rs 20,000/month. Every 5-year delay roughly doubles the required monthly SIP to reach Rs 1 crore.
